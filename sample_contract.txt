SERVICE AGREEMENT

THIS SERVICE AGREEMENT ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 1, 2024 (the "Effective Date"), by and between TechCorp Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Service Provider"), and ClientCorp LLC, a Texas limited liability company ("Client").

1. SERVICES

1.1 Service Provider shall provide professional software development and consulting services ("Services") as detailed in the Statement of Work attached as Exhibit A.

1.2 All Services shall be performed in accordance with industry best practices and professional standards.

1.3 Service Provider shall assign qualified personnel to perform the Services. Client may request replacement of Service Provider personnel upon reasonable notice if performance is unsatisfactory.

2. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS

2.1 Client shall pay Service Provider a fee of $50,000 per month for the Services rendered under this Agreement, payable in arrears within thirty (30) days of invoice receipt.

2.2 Invoices shall be submitted by Service Provider on the first business day of each month covering services rendered in the preceding month.

2.3 All payments shall be made via bank transfer to the account specified in writing by Service Provider.

2.4 Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower.

3. TERM AND TERMINATION

3.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of twelve (12) months ("Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

3.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause upon ninety (90) days written notice to the other party.

3.3 Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice thereof.

3.4 Upon any termination, Client shall pay Service Provider for all Services rendered through the termination date, plus any reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred.

4. CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 Each party ("Disclosing Party") may disclose to the other party ("Receiving Party") certain confidential and proprietary information, including but not limited to source code, documentation, and business information ("Confidential Information").

4.2 The Receiving Party shall: (a) keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential; (b) not disclose it to third parties without prior written consent; (c) use it solely for purposes of performing or evaluating the Services under this Agreement.

4.3 Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known to Receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information.

4.4 Confidential Information shall be protected for a period of five (5) years from the date of disclosure.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

5.1 All pre-existing intellectual property of Service Provider ("Service Provider IP") shall remain the exclusive property of Service Provider.

5.2 All work product created specifically for Client under this Agreement ("Work Product") shall be owned by Client, provided all fees and expenses have been paid in full.

5.3 Client hereby grants Service Provider a non-exclusive, perpetual license to use any methodologies, tools, or processes developed during the performance of Services for other clients.

5.4 Service Provider shall retain ownership of any improvements, modifications, or derivative works to the Work Product not specifically funded by Client.

6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1 NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION LOST PROFITS, LOSS OF REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

6.2 EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID BY CLIENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO LIABILITY.

6.3 The liability limitations in this Section 6 shall not apply to: (a) breaches of confidentiality obligations; (b) indemnification obligations; (c) either party's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

7. WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

7.1 Service Provider warrants that: (a) it has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) the Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner; (c) it will comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

7.2 DISCLAIMER: EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT, SERVICE PROVIDER MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING ANY WARRANTY OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.

7.3 Client warrants that it has the authority to enter into this Agreement and that execution does not violate any other agreement or obligation.

8. INDEMNIFICATION

8.1 Service Provider shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Client from any third-party claims that the Work Product or Services infringe any patent, copyright, or trade secret rights, provided Client: (a) promptly notifies Service Provider of the claim; (b) grants Service Provider sole control of defense and settlement.

8.2 If the Work Product is held to infringe, Service Provider shall, at its option and expense: (a) obtain the right for Client to continue using it; (b) replace or modify it to be non-infringing; (c) if neither option is commercially practical, terminate this Agreement and refund fees paid for the infringing Work Product.

8.3 Client shall indemnify Service Provider against claims that Client's material or specifications provided to Service Provider infringe third-party rights.

9. INSURANCE

9.1 Service Provider shall maintain, at its own expense, professional liability insurance with minimum coverage of $1,000,000 per claim and $2,000,000 aggregate.

9.2 Service Provider shall provide evidence of insurance coverage upon request.

9.3 Service Provider's insurance shall not limit its indemnification obligations hereunder.

10. FORCE MAJEURE

10.1 Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform any obligation under this Agreement due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemics, or government action ("Force Majeure Event").

10.2 The affected party shall: (a) provide prompt notice to the other party; (b) use reasonable efforts to mitigate and resolve the Force Majeure Event; (c) resume performance as soon as practicable.

10.3 If a Force Majeure Event prevents performance for more than sixty (60) days, either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND GOVERNING LAW

11.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

11.2 Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be subject to good faith negotiation between senior executives of each party.

11.3 If negotiation fails, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in New York, New York, in accordance with the Commercial Arbitration Rules.

11.4 Each party waives any right to jury trial and any right to bring claims on a class action basis.

12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, including Exhibit A, constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior understandings, whether written or oral.

12.2 Amendments: No amendment is valid unless in writing and signed by both parties.

12.3 Severability: If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

12.4 Assignment: Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, except Service Provider may assign to an affiliate.

12.5 Notices: All notices shall be in writing and delivered personally, by overnight courier, or by certified mail to the addresses listed below.

12.6 Waiver: No waiver of any provision or breach shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or breach.

12.7 Counterparts: This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all together shall constitute one instrument.
